# Sample English Document

## English Grammar - Verb Tenses

### Present Simple

**Form**: Subject + base verb (+ s/es for 3rd person singular)

**Uses**:
- Habitual actions: "I walk to school every day."
- General truths: "Water boils at 100°C."
- Scheduled events: "The train leaves at 8 AM."

**Examples**:
- She works in a hospital.
- They play football on Saturdays.
- The sun rises in the east.

### Present Continuous (Progressive)

**Form**: Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

**Uses**:
- Actions happening now: "I am studying English."
- Temporary situations: "She is living in Paris this year."
- Future arrangements: "We are meeting tomorrow."

**Examples**:
- He is reading a book.
- They are watching TV right now.
- It is raining outside.

### Past Simple

**Form**: Subject + verb-ed (regular) or irregular past form

**Uses**:
- Completed actions in the past: "I visited London last year."
- Series of past actions: "She opened the door, entered, and sat down."

**Regular verbs**: walk → walked, play → played
**Irregular verbs**: go → went, see → saw, have → had

**Examples**:
- He finished his homework yesterday.
- They went to the cinema last night.
- I saw that movie three times.

### Past Continuous

**Form**: Subject + was/were + verb-ing

**Uses**:
- Actions in progress at a specific time in the past
- Background action when another action happened

**Examples**:
- I was sleeping when you called.
- They were playing chess at 7 PM yesterday.
- While she was cooking, he was cleaning.

### Present Perfect

**Form**: Subject + have/has + past participle

**Uses**:
- Actions completed at an unspecified time: "I have visited France."
- Actions with present results: "She has lost her keys."
- Experiences: "Have you ever been to Japan?"

**Examples**:
- I have finished my work.
- He has lived here for ten years.
- They have never eaten sushi.

### Future Simple

**Form**: Subject + will + base verb

**Uses**:
- Predictions: "It will rain tomorrow."
- Spontaneous decisions: "I'll help you with that."
- Promises: "I will call you later."

**Examples**:
- She will arrive at 6 PM.
- They will win the game.
- I will be there on time.

## Practice Exercises

1. Change to past simple: "I (see) him yesterday."
2. Use present perfect: "She (visit) Paris three times."
3. Form a question: "They are studying English." → "___?"

### Time Expressions

**Present Simple**: always, usually, often, sometimes, never
**Past Simple**: yesterday, last week, ago, in 2020
**Present Perfect**: already, yet, just, ever, never, since, for
**Future**: tomorrow, next week, in the future
